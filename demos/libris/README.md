# Libris research demos

These are **real, unedited-in-substance research reports** produced by
[Libris](../../docs/plans/libris-research-agent.md) — Charon's autonomous
multi-agent research system. Each was generated by giving Libris a single
frontier science question and letting a research agent investigate it against
the live scholarly literature (arXiv, Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex), grade every
claim for confidence and evidence strength, and write a structured report. A
second agent (the judge) then critiqued it.

Every citation was checked against CrossRef/arXiv before publishing — **27 of 27
sources across the three reports resolve to real papers with matching titles**
(no hallucinated references). Any source that could not be verified is flagged in
the report rather than removed.

Open the `.html` files in a browser — each is a single self-contained page

| [Gut microbiome & neurodegeneration](./gut-microbiome-neurodegeneration.html) | Does the gut microbiome **causally** drive neurodegenerative disease, or merely correlate? | 8 (8 ✓) | 8 (7 high / 1 med) | 3 topics, 1 contradicting claim |

Each was **why-it's-a-good-test** chosen for a live scientific controversy:
skill learning is textbook dopaminergic RL while the language case is contested;
microbiome causality is dominated by mouse models while human data is
correlational; and aging-clock causality is unresolved with thin interventional
evidence. In every case the report separates what is well-established from what
is weak or contested rather than flattening it into a confident summary.

## How to read a report

Every factual claim is rendered as a card with three independent epistemic
signals:

- **Confidence** (high / medium / low) — how likely the claim is *true* given the evidence.
- **Stance** (supports / contradicts) — whether the cited source argues *for* or *against* the claim. Contradicting claims get a red border.
- **Evidence grade** (strong / moderate / weak / anecdotal / theoretical / contested) — the *methodological strength* of the underlying evidence, independent of confidence. A meta-analysis and a single mouse study can both make you "confident," but they are not the same grade of evidence.

When supporting and contradicting claims name the same entity, Libris flags it as
a **contested** topic. Each claim links to a numbered citation in the report's
sources panel, tagged by type (paper / repo / doc / web) and credibility.

## How these were generated

```bash
# From a Charon checkout, with a provider configured (/setup):
#   the reports were produced by driving one focused topic per report through
#   a real Libris researcher + judge pass, then rendering with:
python apps/core-daemon/libris_report.py <operation_dir> --out report.html
```

The renderer (`apps/core-daemon/libris_report.py`) is **presentation only** — it
never invents content. Every claim card and citation traces to a row the
research agents actually saved during the run.

## Honest caveats

- These are AI-generated research reports. They are a **well-sourced starting
  point, not a substitute** for reading the primary literature or expert review.
- The epistemic grades are assigned by the research agent (and checked by the
  judge), not by an independent meta-analysis. Treat them as the agent's
  calibrated opinion, surfaced transparently — which is the point.
- Human verification covered citation existence and that each source broadly
  says what the claim attributes to it; it was not a line-by-line fact-check of
  every number.
